Reporting Standards

Methodology
Statement.

This document outlines the principles, boundaries, and calculation methods used by Titan Global to monitor and report its environmental performance. We adhere to the Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Protocol Corporate Standard.

Contents

Organizational Boundaries Scope Definitions Emission Factors Data Quality & Audit

1. Organizational Boundaries

Titan Global applies the "Operational Control" approach for GHG emissions accounting.

Inclusions: All construction sites, regional distribution centers, permanent offices, and majority-owned concessions (airports and toll roads) where Titan Global manages daily operations.

Exclusions: Joint ventures where Titan Global is a minority partner with no operational control are excluded from Scope 1 and 2 but are accounted for in Scope 3 (Category 15: Investments).

2. Scope Definitions & Data Sources

We categorize our emissions into three scopes to ensure comprehensive coverage across our value chain.

Scope 1 (Direct Emissions)

Fuel consumption for the group's logistics fleet, heavy machinery, and heating oil in temporary worksite facilities. Data is collected via fuel invoices and telemetric meters on machinery.

Scope 2 (Indirect Energy)

Purchased electricity and steam for offices and permanent industrial sites. Reported using both location-based (grid average) and market-based (RECs/PPAs) methods.

Scope 3 (Value Chain)

The most significant category for Titan Global. Includes "embodied carbon" in purchased materials (steel, concrete), logistics by third-party carriers, and the use of sold products.

3. Emission Factors

To convert raw energy data into CO₂ equivalents (CO₂e), Titan Global utilizes the most recent factors from internationally recognized databases:

IEA Factors

Electricity emission factors for countries outside Western Europe and North America.

DEFRA / BEIS

Standardized factors for air travel, freight logistics, and stationary combustion.

ICE Database

Embodied carbon factors for construction materials (Inventory of Carbon and Energy).

Ecoinvent

Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) data for specialized industrial processes.

4. Data Quality & Audit

All sustainability data is managed via the **Titan Vanguard** digital platform, which consolidates inputs from over 450 global projects.

  • Internal Control: Quarterly reviews of regional energy dashboards.
  • External Assurance: Our Scope 1 and 2 data is audited annually by a third-party independent body with a "Limited Assurance" level.
  • Base Year: 2024 has been established as the base year for all 2030 targets. Adjustments are made in the event of acquisitions or divestments exceeding a 5% threshold.
