Titan Glossary

A comprehensive guide to the technical terms, corporate acronyms, and industry terminology used across the Titan Global ecosystem.

A B C D E F G H I

Categories

All Terms Technical Financial Sustainability
A

Alignment of Interests

The corporate principle ensuring that Titan’s long-term strategic goals mirror the benefits of its shareholders, employees, and the environment.

Autoconsumption

The production and immediate use of renewable energy on a construction site or within a concession, reducing reliance on the external grid.

B

BIM (Building Information Modeling)

An intelligent 3D model-based process that gives architecture, engineering, and construction professionals the insight and tools to more efficiently plan, design, construct, and manage buildings and infrastructure.

Brownfield Development

Construction projects located on land previously used for industrial purposes, often requiring environmental remediation handled by Titan Energy Solutions.

C

Circular Construction

An approach to building that focuses on the reuse of materials and the reduction of waste throughout the entire lifecycle of a project.

Concessions

Long-term contracts under which Titan finances, builds, and operates public infrastructure such as airports, motorways, and stadiums.

D

Decarbonization Path

Titan’s multi-year roadmap aimed at achieving a 40% reduction in direct CO2 emissions by 2030 across all construction activities.

E

EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction)

A common form of contracting arrangement where the contractor is responsible for all activities from design to the final delivery of the asset.

Embedded Carbon

The total greenhouse gas emissions generated during the raw material extraction, transport, and manufacturing of construction components.

Energy Performance Contract

A partnership aimed at improving energy efficiency in a building where the investment is repaid through the resulting energy savings.

F

Facility Management

The integrated management of the technical and administrative services required for the smooth operation of built infrastructure.

Free Cash Flow

A key financial metric representing the cash generated by Titan Global operations after accounting for capital expenditures.

G

Geothermal Energy

A renewable energy source harnessed from the Earth's internal heat, utilized by Titan Energy Solutions for carbon-neutral heating.

Greenfield Project

A project executed on previously undeveloped land, as opposed to a brownfield redevelopment.

I

Inductive Charging (Infrastructure)

Wireless charging technology integrated into road surfaces to power electric vehicles while in motion.

Inland Empire Project

Strategic industrial development initiatives within Southern California's logistics corridor, focusing on sustainable manufacturing hubs.

IoT (Internet of Things)

The network of physical objects embedded with sensors and software to exchange data with other systems during building operation.

L

Lifecycle Assessment (LCA)

A systematic analysis of the environmental impact of a building across its entire lifespan, from "cradle to grave."

Low-Carbon Concrete

Innovative cement mixes that utilize recycled materials or carbon-capture tech to reduce the carbon footprint of structural frames.

M

Mixed-Use Development

Urban planning that blends residential, commercial, and industrial uses into one cohesive, walkable project area.

Modular Construction

A process in which a building is constructed off-site, under controlled plant conditions, before being assembled on-site.

P

PPP (Public-Private Partnership)

A long-term cooperative arrangement between two or more public and private sectors for the delivery of infrastructure services.

Photovoltaic Infrastructure

Solar energy systems integrated into building envelopes or infrastructure assets to generate on-site electricity.

R

Remediation

The process of removing contaminants from soil or groundwater at industrial sites to return the land to a safe state for redevelopment.

Retrofitting

Adding new technology or features to older buildings to improve energy efficiency or meet modern environmental standards.

S

Scope 1, 2, and 3 Emissions

The three categories of greenhouse gas emissions: direct (Scope 1), indirect from purchased energy (Scope 2), and all other indirect value chain emissions (Scope 3).

Smart Grid

An electricity network that uses digital technology to monitor and manage the transport of electricity from all generation sources to meet demand.
